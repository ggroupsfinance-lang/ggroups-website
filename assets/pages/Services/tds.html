Frequently Asked Questions – TDS & TCS Compliance

What is the due date for TDS return filing in India?

TDS returns must be filed quarterly. The due dates are typically 31st July, 31st October, 31st January, and 31st May for Q1 to Q4 respectively. Late filing attracts penalties under the Income Tax Act.

What is the difference between TDS and TCS?

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is deducted by the payer while making specific payments such as salary, rent, or professional fees. TCS (Tax Collected at Source) is collected by the seller from the buyer at the time of sale of specified goods.

What happens if TDS is not deposited on time?

Non-deposit or delayed deposit of TDS attracts interest, late filing fees, and penalties. In serious cases, prosecution provisions may also apply. Timely compliance helps avoid unnecessary financial and legal risks.

Which TDS return forms need to be filed?

The commonly filed TDS return forms include:
• Form 24Q – Salary payments
• Form 26Q – Non-salary payments to residents
• Form 27Q – Payments to non-residents
• Form 27EQ – TCS returns

What is Section 194Q and 206C(1H)?

Section 194Q requires buyers to deduct TDS on purchase of goods exceeding prescribed limits, while Section 206C(1H) requires sellers to collect TCS on sale of goods beyond the specified turnover threshold.

Can TDS returns be corrected after filing?

Yes. TDS returns can be revised to correct PAN errors, challan mismatches, short deductions, or other inaccuracies. Correction statements must be filed promptly to avoid defaults and notices.
